Mexican Street Corn Salad

Makes 4 servings.

Ingredients

For the Salad:
  • 1 bunch green kale, leaves cut off the stem and cut into bite size pieces
  • 2 ears of corn
  • ½ cup feta, crumbled
  • ¼ cup red onion, chopped
  • 1-2 avocados, sliced or diced
  • Crushed tortilla chips to add at the end
  • 1 tsp olive oil (for massaging the kale)
  • ½ of a lime, juiced (for massaging the kale)*
  • Hot sauce to add a kick at the end as garnish
For the cilantro lime dressing:
  • ½ cup plain greek yogurt
  • ¼ cup fresh lime juice*
  • ¼ cup cilantro, chopped (leaves & stems are fine)
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp smoked paprika
  • ¼ tsp chili powder

Instructions

  • Bring a pot of water to a boil on the stove and remove the husks from your corn cobs. Once the water begins to boil, add your corn to the pot, cover it and boil for 1-2 minutes. Remove the corn from the pot and let the cobs cool completely before cutting off the kernels. You could also grill the corn to make this extra dope.

  • Next, place the kale leaves in a large bowl and drizzle them with your oil and lime juice. Rub the leaves together with your hands for 5 minutes or until the kale begins to soften and shrinks in size.

  • Then add your corn kernels, red onion, feta and avocado to the bowl and toss with your desired amount of dressing.

  • Top with crushed tortilla chips and perhaps a hit of hot suace right before serving.
